This guidance explains how local authorities ( LAs ) should organise monitoring visits to schools administering the 2016 key stage 2 ( KS2 ) national curriculum tests and the phonics screening check.

Local authorities responsibilities

LAs have a statutory responsibility to monitor the administration of the KS2 national curriculum tests and phonics screening check. Monitoring visits help us to ensure that:

  • the security and confidentiality of test and check materials are maintained
  • the KS2 tests and phonics screening check are administered correctly and consistently

This year, when monitoring the administration of the KS2 tests, local authority monitoring visitors will also be asked to review the security of key stage 1 ( KS1 ) test materials, where applicable. They are not expected to observe the actual administration of the KS1 tests.

Monitoring visits also give headteachers a formal opportunity to demonstrate how their school is administering the KS2 tests and phonics screening check and maintaining the security of the KS1 test materials.

Academies have been asked to choose whether to be monitored by their current LA , another LA or the Standards and Testing Agency ( STA ).

Independent schools and academies that have chosen not to be monitored by an LA will be monitored by a monitoring visits agency on our behalf.

STA may also make monitoring visits to maintained schools.

References to academies in this guidance include free schools, as in law they are recognised as academies.

Managing monitoring visits

We recommend that each LA gives responsibility to 1 person to act as their monitoring visits co-ordinator. The monitoring visits co-ordinator should:

  • arrange monitoring visits
  • select monitoring visitors
  • brief monitoring visitors on the purpose, scope and procedures involved in conducting monitoring visits
  • provide monitoring visitors with the information they will need for the visits
  • process completed monitoring visits forms

Arranging monitoring visits

Providing information to schools

Before any monitoring visits take place, you may want to contact all schools within your authority to explain the purpose of the visits. You may give the names of individuals involved in the visits, but you must not identify in advance any specific schools that will be visited.

You should make schools aware of this information and the guidance for key stage 2 and the phonics screening check . This is so that schools know what to expect if they receive a monitoring visit.

If schools receive a visit and wish to confirm the identity of their visitor(s) they should contact you, for LA visitors, or the national curriculum assessments helpline if the visitor is a member of STA ’s staff.

Independent schools can also contact the national curriculum assessments helpline if they wish to confirm the identity of a visitor from our monitoring visits agency.

Key stage 2 monitoring visits

At least 10% of maintained schools administering the tests in your authority must receive a visit. This includes:

  • participating pupil referral units or special schools
  • academies that have chosen to be monitored by your LA
  • a sample of schools which we will identify

We will give you details of the schools which we want you to visit in early April.

You should not arrange for more than 1 visit to an individual school unless we have raised specific concerns with you.

We recommend that approximately:

  • 10% of visits take place during the week commencing 2 May
  • 80% of visits are carried out during the test period, Monday 9 May to Thursday 12 May
  • 10% of visits take place immediately after the test period, from Friday 13 May

Phonics screening check monitoring visits

At least 10% of schools administering the phonics screening check in your authority must receive a visit. This includes:

  • participating pupil referral units or special schools
  • academies that have chosen to be monitored by your LA
  • maintained nursery schools with registered pupils who will reach the age of 6 before the end of the school year

You should not arrange for more than 1 visit to an individual school unless we have raised specific concerns with you.

We recommend that approximately:

  • 10% of visits take place during the week commencing 6 June
  • 80% of visits are carried out during the phonics screening check period, Monday 13 June to Friday 17 June
  • 10% of visits take place immediately after the phonics screening check period, from Monday 20 June

Preparing monitoring visitors

You are responsible for making sure your LA ’s monitoring visitors:

  • understand how to carry out a monitoring visit
  • understand how to complete and return the monitoring visit forms
  • are familiar with the relevant administration guidance
  • have the necessary background information for each visit

You should brief them fully before they carry out any visits.

Key stage 2 monitoring visitors

KS2 monitoring visitors should understand the guidance on carrying out KS2 monitoring visits . This explains what they need to do before, during and after a monitoring visit.

They must be familiar with the 2016 test administration guidance, in particular the:

  • KS2 Assessment and reporting arrangements
  • KS2 test administration guidance
  • KS1 test administration guidance (in particular the section about maintaining the security of the test materials)
  • guidance on administering modified versions of the KS2 tests
  • information on access arrangements for the KS2 tests
  • Attendance register and test script dispatch instructions for the KS2 tests
  • Guidance on the security of key stage 2 tests and phonics screening check materials

Before each monitoring visit you must give your monitoring visitors:

  • copies of the KS2 monitoring visit form
  • details of the number of pupils being assessed at the school
  • reports from the NCA tools website showing whether the school is using additional time for any tests, opening test packs early, administering any tests at another location or using timetable variations
  • information to show which test materials the school has received for KS2 , and where appropriate also for KS1

In April we will email your NCA tools super user a spreadsheet which summarises the materials which each school has received. Please note: you will need your registered email address and password to access the NCA tools website .

Phonics screening check monitoring visitors

Phonics screening check monitoring visitors should understand the guidance on carrying out phonics screening check monitoring visits . This explains what they need to do before, during and after a monitoring visit.

They must be familiar with the 2016 check administration guidance, in particular the:

  • KS1 test administration guidance
  • KS1 Assessment and reporting arrangements
  • Key stage 1: phonics screening check administration guidance
  • Guidance on the security of key stage 2 tests and phonics screening check materials
  • year 1 phonics screening check training video

Before each monitoring visit you must give your monitoring visitors:

  • copies of the phonics screening check monitoring visit form
  • details of the number of pupils being assessed at the school including any year 2 pupils who are expected to take the check
  • information to show which check materials the school has received
  • copies of the 2016 check materials (these will be available to download from NCA tools website from Monday 13 June)

You should review the data submitted by schools in 2015 in order to identify pupils in year 2 who should be taking the check. In May we will email your NCA tools super user a spreadsheet which summarises the materials which each school has received.

Monitoring visit forms

There are separate forms for key stage 2 and phonics screening check monitoring visits.

Make sure that your monitoring visitors understand that they must complete all the questions for each school they visit. If an ‘X’, ‘N’ or ‘P’ is entered on the monitoring visit form, or any other irregularities are noticed during the visit, you must record details of the issue and any actions taken on the form. This will help us identify instances where further investigation by you and / or STA is required.

Processing monitoring visit forms

If a monitoring visitor has placed an ‘X’, ‘N’ or ‘P’ in any box on the monitoring visit form, they should discuss the issue with you immediately. You should then scan and email the monitoring visit form and any annotated delivery notes (where applicable) to STA at STA .Maladministration@education.gsi.gov.uk.

Our maladministration team will follow up all instances where a monitoring visitor records an ‘X’, ‘N’ or ‘P’ on a monitoring visit form. If we investigate an allegation of maladministration and have doubts about whether a pupil’s answers represent their own independent and unaided work, test or phonics screening check results may be amended or annulled.

If no irregularities were identified during the visit, monitoring visitors must send their completed forms to you by the following dates:

  • Friday 20 May for KS2 visits
  • Friday 24 June for phonics screening check visits

You must send all completed forms, including those already emailed to STA , to:


STA ’s monitoring visits agency
Quality & Customers Standards Unit
AQA
Stag Hill House
Guildford
Surrey, GU2 7XJ









The deadlines for sending forms to our monitoring visits agency are:

  • Tuesday 24 May for KS2 forms
  • Wednesday 29 June for phonics screening check forms

You don’t need to send delivery notes to our monitoring visits agency unless there are issues of concern noted on the monitoring visit form.

You should consider keeping copies of completed monitoring visit forms for your own reference.
