Introduction

As reformed qualifications are introduced in England, legacy GCSEs, AS and A levels are being withdrawn.

We have now taken decisions on the re-sit arrangements for legacy GCSEs, AS and A levels.

This document explains - for legacy GCSE, AS and A level subjects:

  • when the last courses begin (assuming a two-year course of study for GCSEs, and a coupled two-year course of study for AS and A level, with AS exams taken at the end of year 12 or 13, and A level exams taken at the end of year 13)
  • when the last exams will take place, and the arrangements for students to re-sit their exams
  • which (if any) reformed qualification will replace it
  • if a subject is not being replaced, the reason why

The tables below do not currently include information about modern foreign languages (other than French, German and Spanish). This is because final decisions have not been taken about when these subjects will be reformed. We will update these tables as soon as more information is available
