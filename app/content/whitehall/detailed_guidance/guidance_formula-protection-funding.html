Introduction

This information is for institutions that received formula protection funding ( FPF ) in academic year 2016 to 2017. We will only pay FPF to those institutions that received FPF in 2016 to 2017.

We introduced FPF in academic year 2013 to 2014 to moderate the impact of the funding formula changes. FPF enables institutions to manage and plan for the change to funding per student. We originally committed to providing FPF for 3 years, up to and including academic year 2015 to 2016 and then to review it.

We confirmed in January 2016 that FPF will be phased out over 6 years. We made the first reduction in 2016 to 2017 and the last payments will be made to institutions in 2020 to 2021.

We explain how FPF fits within the 16 to 19 funding formula within our funding guidance books .

Purpose

The purpose of this information is:

  • to detail how FPF will be applied in the 2017 to 2018 academic year, and
  • to support institutions to understand how it will apply to their own allocation

Principles

We will pay FPF in 2017 to 2018 on the following principles:

  • we will only pay FPF to those institutions that received FPF in 2016 to 2017
  • the reduction in FPF per student in 2017 to 2018 will be the same as the reduction per student applied in 2016 to 2017
  • the number of students that attract FPF will not be greater than it was in 2015 to 2016
  • the calculation of FPF will be reviewed each academic year

Calculating FPF for the 2017 to 2018 academic year

We will calculate FPF as shown in the 4 steps below.

Step 1

We will bring forward the following values from previous years:

  • the 2016 to 2017 FPF per student
  • the reduction in FPF per student used in calculating 2016 to 2017 FPF per student
  • the number of funded students in 2015 to 2016
Figures brought forward from 2016 to 2017 Institution A Institution B Institution C
FPF per student in 2016 to 2017 (A) £270 £550 £60
Reduction in FPF applied in calculating the 2016 to 2017 allocation (B) £100 £110 £90
Number of funded students in 2015 to 2016 (C) 3000 250 600

Step 2

We will reduce the 2017 to 2018 FPF per student by the same amount that we used when calculating your 2016 to 2017 FPF (figure B from step 1).

If this gives a figure above zero, that is the FPF per student in 2017 to 2018. If the figure is zero or lower, then no FPF will be paid in 2017 to 2018 or subsequent years.

Calculation of FPF per student in 2017 to 2018 Institution A Institution B Institution C
FPF per student in 2016 to 2017 (A) £270 £550 £60
Reduction applied in 2016 to 2017 (B) £100 £110 £90
FPF per student in 2017 to 2018 – A-B with a minimum of zero (D) £170 £440 £0

Step 3

We will compare the 2017 to 2018 student numbers to the number of funded students in 2015 to 2016. The student numbers eligible for FPF in 2017 to 2018 will be the lower of these 2 figures.

Calculation of number of students eligible for FPF in 2017 to 2018 Institution A Institution B Institution C
Number of funded students in 2015 to 2016 (C) 3000 250 Not applicable – no FPF in 2017 to 2018
Number of students eligible for FPF in 2017 to 2018 (F) 3000 240 Not applicable – no FPF in 2017 to 2018
Total FPF in 2017 to 2018 – DxF (G) £510,000 £105,600 Not applicable – no FPF in 2017 to 2018

Step 4

We will calculate the total FPF by multiplying the final FPF per student by the eligible student numbers.

FPF per student in 2017 to 2018 (D) £170 £440 Not applicable – no FPF in 2017 to 2018
Number of students eligible for FPF in 2017 to 2018 (F) 3000 240 Not applicable – no FPF in 2017 to 2018
Total FPF in 2017 to 2018 – DxF (G) £510,000 £105,600 Not applicable – no FPF in 2017 to 2018
