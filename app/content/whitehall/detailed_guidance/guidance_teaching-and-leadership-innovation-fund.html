Overview

The Teaching and Leadership Innovation Fund is a 3-year investment worth £75 million, which will support high-quality professional development for teachers and school leaders in areas of the country that need it most.

The aim of the fund is to improve pupil outcomes in priority areas by:

  • improving the provision of teachers’ continuing professional development (CPD) and leadership development in challenging areas and challenging schools

  • stimulating the demand for provision of teachers’ CPD and leadership development in challenging areas and challenging schools

  • supporting the development of a sustainable market in CPD and leadership development that will be capable of becoming self-funding in the future

The Secretary of State announced this as part of the social mobility package in October 2016 and announced the first bidding round in February 2017.

Resources from our supplier information events are now available on contracts finder . This includes a copy of the presentation and recordings of the 2 WebEx sessions held on 3 March.

What’s involved

We are inviting potential providers to apply for funding , and expect bids to have an overall focus on improving the quality of teaching and leadership in challenging areas and schools.

All bids must have a strong focus on delivering the majority of their provision in opportunity areas or category 5 and 6 areas . Your bid must also clearly demonstrate how you will focus on the most challenging schools.

Opportunity areas

The opportunity areas are: Blackpool, Bradford, Derby, Doncaster, Fenland and East Cambridgeshire, Hastings, Ipswich, Norwich, Oldham, Scarborough, Stoke-on-Trent and West Somerset.
