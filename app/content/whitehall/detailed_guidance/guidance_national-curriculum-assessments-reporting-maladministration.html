Maladministration is any act that:

  • affects the integrity, security or confidentiality of the national curriculum assessments
  • could lead to results that do not reflect children’s unaided work or actual abilities

Maladministration can lead to changes to, or annulment of, results for whole cohorts, groups of pupils or individual pupils.

How to report a concern

If you are concerned about how the phonics screening check, key stage 2 ( KS2 ) tests or teacher assessment has been carried out, you should:

  • contact the national curriculum assessments helpline
  • email maladministration team

Examples of maladministration

In the KS2 tests or phonics screening check maladministration could be:

  • incorrect opening of test papers or check materials
  • pupils cheating
  • pupils being over-aided
  • resitting of a test or check
  • changes made to pupils’ test scripts outside of test conditions
  • incorrect reporting of pupils’ check scores

Maladministration of teacher assessment could be:

  • inflation or deflation of pupils’ teacher assessment judgements to influence school assessment outcomes
  • submission of different data to that agreed following local authority ( LA ) moderation
  • scaffolding of work or over-aiding in tests or tasks that are used as evidence to justify teacher assessment levels

How national curriculum assessments should be carried out

We have published information which tells teachers how to administer the KS2 tests and phonics screening check . We have also published guidance to support making teacher assessment judgements.

Maladministration investigations

We will follow our maladministration investigation procedures when examining any matter brought to our attention relating to maladministration. Our maladministration team works closely with LAs during its investigations.
