This hospitality industry is one of the largest employers in the UK and includes everything from hotels and silver service restaurants, to bars and nightclubs.

Training opportunities

This level 4 higher apprenticeship will provide you with nationally recognised qualifications and skills in a sector that is well known for having fast-track career progression, with management-level positions often being available to committed team members.

To qualify for this apprenticeship, you must demonstrate that you have a lot of experience in supervisory roles, leading a team or being in charge of a department

Apprenticeship pay and holidays

Types of apprenticeships

This is a level 4 higher apprenticeship. You could train in roles like:

  • deputy general manager
  • front office manager
  • operations manager
  • unit manager

Apply for a hospitality management apprenticeship

You can become an apprentice in England if you’re:

  • 16 or over
  • eligible to work in England
  • not in full-time education
