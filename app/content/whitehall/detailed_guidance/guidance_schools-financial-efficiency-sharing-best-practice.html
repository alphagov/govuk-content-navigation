Introduction

Peer support within the sector is one of the best drivers of improvement. There is already some excellent practice in schools and this page will help you find and use it. Below are links to sector organisations providing information, training and support for schools, and an invitation to submit your own case studies to help other schools.

Sector organisations

National Association of Head Teachers (NAHT)

The National Association of Head Teachers (NAHT) is an independent trade union and professional association whose services include direct support, training and development on finance and resource management.

Association of School and College Leaders ( ASCL )

The Association of School and College Leaders ( ASCL ) is a leading professional body representing school, college and system leaders across the UK.

National Association of School Business Management ( NASBM )

The National Association of School Business Management ( NASBM ) supports and represents the school business management profession.

National Governors’ Association ( NGA )

The National Governors’ Association ( NGA ) is an independent charity that aims to support and promote good governance in schools.

Chartered Institute of Public Finance and Accountancy ( CIPFA )

CIPFA is the professional body for people in public finance. Their services include an academies hub which provides expert guidance on accounting, financial management and business matters for academies.

Teaching Schools Council ( TSC )

The Teaching Schools Council ( TSC ) is a body representing all teaching schools which works with system leaders across England to promote an inclusive school-led system.

The schools in this list of teaching schools offer financial management advice and expertise to other schools, which is clearly linked to school improvement strategy.

Their advice is based on practical experience and is distinct from any advice that schools may receive from DfE. Please contact these schools directly if you are interested in receiving support.

Share your good practice

Many schools manage their budgets very well. Our schools financial health case studies illustrate some of the ways in which schools and academies have developed effective financial management practices. They provide examples of excellent practice from which school leaders and governors can learn.

We will produce more case studies on a regular basis, and would welcome any good practice examples or case studies you would like to share. Please use our Schools financial efficiency: case study template ( MS Word Document , 83.6KB ) and submit this to us using either of the enquiry forms below.

Questions, comments or suggestions

We’d like to hear from you if you have any questions, comments or suggestions about this page or any of the schools financial efficiency content. This includes how we can improve the content and what other materials you would find helpful.

If you are an academy, local authority or post-16 provider, please use the EFA enquiry form including schools financial efficiency in the subject title.

For all other institutions, including maintained schools, please use the DfE enquiry form including schools financial efficiency in the subject title.
