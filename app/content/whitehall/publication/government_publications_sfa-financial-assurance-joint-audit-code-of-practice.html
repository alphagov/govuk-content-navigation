How the EFA and SFA ensure FE colleges are accountable and have robust audit arrangements. The Joint Audit Code of Practice (JACOP) sets out a common standard for the provision of assurance in relation to funding of post-16 providers.

Details

This Joint Audit Code of Practice sets out the specific requirements for the assurance, accountability and audit arrangements for providers of post-16 education and training and the broad framework in which they should operate.

JACOP is relevant to the funding bodies, providers of apprenticeships, young people’s education and adult skills and their external auditors / reporting accountants.

It should be read alongside any agreements setting out the conditions of funding, including any contract with Skills Funding Agency (SFA) or Education Funding Agency (EFA).

This version of JACOP is relevant to accounting periods ending on or after 31 July 2016. It will be updated by SFA and EFA as required.
