Guidance for teachers and teaching assistants administering the key stage 2 tests to pupils with a hearing impairment.

The following general guidance will help you prepare for administering the key stage 2 tests. You should follow it to make sure pupils with a hearing impairment can access the tests properly and are not at a disadvantage. You should use this guidance together with the:

  • 2015 test administration guidance ( TAG )
  • printed instructions that accompany the test papers

The printed instructions include information about what you should do before, during and after each test. Please follow them carefully in order to avoid accusations of maladministration. The instructions aren’t on GOV.UK because they contain information which must stay confidential until the test period is over.

English tests

English reading test

You may only use and give explanations, to convey the general instructions. This includes information on the front cover of the test papers and any directions that are not part of the actual questions. For example, ‘These questions are about caves’.

English grammar, punctuation and spelling test

You may only use sign language, and give an explanation:

  • to convey the general instructions
  • where test administrators are instructed to read the prompts to the pupil
  • to read and / or sign back to the pupil any part of his or her response

Spelling task

A communicator or sign language interpreter may be used during the spelling task. However, if the spelling task is presented through sign language, finger spelling mustn’t be used.

Compensatory marks

Pupils who have been granted a compensatory mark for the spelling task are exempt from this component and mustn’t attempt it. If the pupil takes the task, the test script (answer booklet or test paper containing the pupil’s answers) must be sent for marking and their mark will stand. We will not award a compensatory mark.

Levels 3-5 mental mathematics test

A few pupils may need to use modified versions of the mental mathematics test materials. These pupils must take the test in a separate room supported by a communicator or sign language interpreter who has previously worked with them. They must use the standard answer sheet.

We have made the following changes to the procedures for administering the modified version of the test.

Instructions for administrators

The ‘Mental mathematics scripts for pupils with a hearing impairment’ includes full instructions and the scripts for administering the modified mental mathematics test. A printed copy will be in your test materials pack. Use the most appropriate administrator script for the pupils being tested. For example, for pupils who have some residual hearing and who are used to lip-reading or who use supported English communication, the oralist and supported English script may be the most appropriate form of communication. For pupils who are more familiar with British sign language, the British sign language script may be preferred.

Stimulus materials

The test materials include stimulus material for each question. This is available as either flashcards or a CD. You can use these to:

  • assist lip-reading
  • help clarify words which might cause confusion acoustically, for example 13 / 30
  • clarify polysyllabic words
  • convey mathematical words which can’t be signed because an iconographic sign may convey the meaning of the concept being tested

You should adopt whichever support method is closest to normal classroom practice.

You mustn’t use the standard test CD or the standard teacher-read transcript with the stimulus material.

You should give pupils as much additional time as they need to register the content of the stimulus material before asking them to concentrate on you as you read the question. The amount of additional time you may allow will vary depending on the nature and degree of each pupil’s impairment. However, you must make sure that the test response times in the modified scripts are strictly adhered to.

Timings

The time pupils will have to answer each question will increase from 10 seconds, to 20 seconds, and then to 30 seconds as the test progresses through the 3 sections.

We have increased the time so that pupils can process the language, and redirect their attention from the sign language interpreter or communicator and the stimulus material to their answer sheets. This is not to allow them additional response time.

You will need to be able to signal to pupils when the response time for each question starts and finishes. You could use a light or vibration signal. You must ensure that pupils recognise the sign or instruction for putting down their pens at the end of the response time.

Pupils should put down their pens or pencils at the end of the response time for each question. They should then pay you full attention while you show the stimulus material for the next question and read or sign the question.

Compensatory marks

Pupils who have been granted a compensatory mark for the mental mathematics test are exempt from this component and mustn’t attempt it. If the pupil takes the test, the test script (answer booklet or test paper containing the pupil’s answers) must be sent for marking and the mark will stand. We will not award a compensatory mark.

Written mathematics tests

Instructions accompany the test materials for levels 3-5 and level 6 Papers 1 and 2. These describe adaptations which you can make, and suggested rephrasing of words and sentences which may be signed. The instructions highlight any questions where you may need to give the pupil further clarification or exemplification.
