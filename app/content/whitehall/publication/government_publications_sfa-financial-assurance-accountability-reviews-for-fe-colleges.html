Detail

Explains for colleges and training organisations and Agency staff why accountability reviews have been introduced, and their scope and purpose.

This guidance supports colleges and training organisations in preparing for an accountability review, if they have been selected for one. It also provides information for Agency staff who will undertake an accountability review at a selected college or training organisation.

The Agency will undertake reviews at colleges and training organisations where significant risks have been identified.

In normal circumstances, it is unlikely that an accountability review will be undertaken at any non-college training organisation with a contract under Â£10 million.

These accountability reviews will cover arrangements for:

  • governance
  • strategic oversight
  • financial management
  • internal control

Following on from the simplification work that the Agency undertook with the sector in 2012:

  • it is no longer mandatory for providers to complete and submit an annual financial management and control evaluation return to the Agency
  • the SFA will continue undertake reviews to ensure that colleges and training organisations have effective systems in place to manage and monitor the funds given to them
  • the number of reviews will be significantly reduced due to a risk-based approach

Colleges and training organisations can comment on these accountability reviews and how they could be further developed and refined, particularly in respect of their scope, usefulness and content.
