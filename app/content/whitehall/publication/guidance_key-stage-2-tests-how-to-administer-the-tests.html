Guidance for test administrators

Test administrators can be headteachers, teachers or teaching assistants. If you’re involved in administering the key stage 2 tests in May 2015 you should prepare by reading the test administration guidance ( TAG ). This includes information about:

  • the statutory test timetable
  • receiving, checking and securely storing test materials
  • planning for the tests
  • administering standard and modified versions of the levels 3-5 and level 6 tests, including how to deal with issues during the tests
  • completing the attendance register
  • packing and securely storing test scripts
  • returning test scripts for marking

This information builds on the 2015 key stage 2 ‘Assessment and reporting arrangements’ .

A small number of pupils may need access arrangements so they can take part in the tests. You may need to prepare differently for the tests to accommodate a pupil’s specific needs. You should therefore consider whether or not to use additional arrangements before the tests take place. You may also need to make applications or notify us that you are using access arrangements. The planning wallchart shows deadline dates for access arrangements notifications and deadlines.

Please also be aware of the changes introduced this year and how they will affect how you administer the tests.

Guidance for headteachers

You are responsible for ensuring all test administrators are familiar with and comply with all of the test administration guidance. Schools that do not comply could be subject to a maladministration investigation. After the tests have taken place, you must complete and submit the headteacher’s declaration form . This is to confirm that either:

  • your school has administered the tests according to the published guidance or
  • the statements in the form can’t be confirmed and that you’ve raised any issues with us

Changes for 2015

Format of the ‘Test administrators’ guide’

You won’t receive printed copies of the ‘Test administrators guide’. All test administration guidance is available on GOV.UK.

You will receive a printed copy of specific instructions for administering each test in each pack of test papers. You will also receive a printed copy of the instructions for completing attendance registers and packing and returning test scripts for marking. This will be included in your test stationery pack.

You will receive your test materials in the week commencing 27 April.

Online access arrangements notification forms

All access arrangements notification forms must be completed via the ‘Access arrangements’ section of the NCA tools website . Forms must not be printed off and returned with test scripts. Word versions of the forms are no longer available.

All test scripts will be marked onscreen

All tests at all levels will be marked onscreen in 2015. The information to help you prepare for onscreen marking includes guidance on:

  • keeping test scripts in good condition to aid the scanning process
  • where pupils must place their responses within the test papers
  • which pen or pencil to use

Changes to format of answer booklets

The levels 3-5 mental mathematics answer sheet is double sided this year. The front is a cover page with space for the pupil’s name, school and Department for Education number. The answer boxes are on the back of the sheet.

The level 6 English grammar, punctuation and spelling Paper 1: extended task answer booklet has an extra page at the back. If pupils ask for additional paper they should use this space first. Pupils are still only expected to produce up to 2 sides of writing.

English reading test ‘find and copy’ questions

‘Find and copy’ questions in the English reading tests will now ask pupils to retrieve a ‘word or group of words’ rather than a ‘word or phrase’.

Packing test scripts for marking

The packaging for returning test scripts for marking has been simplified. Changes include the introduction of transparent bags for each component of each test, with an external bag for each day. There will also be fewer labels as all scripts will be going to 1 destination. Instructions for packing and returning materials will be included in your stationery pack.
