  • Iteration 4

    Feburary 2017

    Wrote descriptions under taxons as a combination of nouns and verbs
    To have a consistent and effect way to describe what each taxon contains and to support users in making an informed decision when they navigate
    Curated content in the sidebar
    To help users navigating to where they need to go by suggesting in the sidebar content that could be relevant to it

    Research findings

  • Iteration 3: anchor / accordion

    December 2016

    Added more content and taxons at higher levels of the education taxonomy
    To make the prototype closer to reality with the amount of content we have
    Added the Parent topic title link
    To help users orientate themselves
    Tested two different ways to expose content of the long list: anchor and accordion
    The long list broken down by subjects felt easier to skim, but still perceived as very long

    Research findings

  • Iteration 2

    November 2016

    Removed the sections "Most popular' and 'Latest'
    None of the users noticed them without prompting. 'Latest' feels more needed because education users need always to know the latest information to do their job. 'Most popular' feels like a redundant category because users cannot tell who it is for
    Added navigation of higher levels of the education taxonomy
    We want to see how users move across levels of the taxonomy
    Broke down the long list in Key Stage 1 and Key Stage 2 and each of those by subjects
    Users found it frustrating to use and hard to find content they were after
    Added descriptions under each taxon and under each piece of content
    In the taxon case, we want to give an example of the breadth of content each topic contains. In the descriptions case, we want to have an explanation of unclear titles
    Focused on 'Guidance' content and removed 'Research & Analysis'
    We've focussed on guidance to begin with because it's the most viewed content type and the one we know most about
